How I work

Process.
Intentional by design.

very project follows a considered sequence. It’s not a rigid formula, more a way of thinking that keeps decisions accountable and outcomes grounded in the problem rather than the assumption.

Step 01

Understand the brief

In order to build a solution we first have to understand the problem. That means reading between the lines of what's been asked, identifying what hasn't been said, and establishing what success actually looks like.

This stage involves conversations, questions about audience, constraints, existing attempts, and what's been tried before. The goal is to arrive at a shared definition of the problem, create a goal and to build a definition of done.

Stakeholder conversations Problem framing Constraints mapping Audience definition

Step 02

Research and audit

Existing work is directed. For brand and UI projects that means an accessibility audit, a competitive landscape review, and an honest assessment of what's working and what isn't.

This stage always includes a WCAG 2.2 review. Accessibility issues compound over time, so catching them at the audit stage is far less costly than retrofitting them at the end.

WCAG 2.2 audit Competitive review Brand assessment User research
Where AI earns its place: Research and pattern recognition across large sets of feedback or audit data. AI can surface patterns faster than manual review, but the judgement on contextual and scenario based accessibility remains human.

Step 03

Define the system

Good visual design without a system behind it eventually falls apart. So before any interface work begins, the foundational decisions are made and documented: colour tokens, type scale, spacing, interaction states.

For accessibility, contrast ratios are checked and documented. Semantic colour roles are defined. Interaction states — hover, focus, active, disabled — are specified for every interactive component. If it isn't defined here, it will end up an inconsistency later.

Design tokens Contrast ratios Interaction states Type scale Spacing system

Step 04

Design and iterate

With a defined system and a clear problem, design work can move quickly. Iterations are grounded in feedback. Wireframes can conceptualise function quickly before spending time on the high fidelity design.

Every decision should be documentable. If a colour was chosen or a layout was structured a particular way, there's a defendable reason.

Component design Responsive layout Prototyping Feedback loops
Where AI earns its place: Iteration acceleration — exploring variations on a layout or component faster than manual production allows. The brief for each variation is still written by a human who understands the problem. AI isn’t used to design; it executes instructions at speed.

Step 05

Review and validate

Before delivery, the work is checked against the original problem definition. Does it solve what was identified in step one? Has it introduced new accessibility issues? Does it hold up across screen sizes, input methods, and colour modes?

Validation isn't a final polish pass but a structured check against documented criteria and the definition of done.

Accessibility check Cross-device review Brief alignment Dark & light mode

Step 06

Deliver and document

Delivery isn't the end of the process, it's the start of the next one. Digital products require continuous improvement and maintenance.

The Ember case study on this site is an example of this process in action. Researched, documented, built and tested using AI to expedite selected parts of the process.

Token documentation Handoff notes Design system Rationale record
On AI and environmental cost: Every step in this process considers whether AI tooling is appropriate. AI inference has a measurable carbon cost. Where something can be done manually with equivalent quality and speed, it is. The question isn't whether AI can help, it's whether it should.

This process was applied directly to the Ember case study — from the initial accessibility audit through to the documented design system and live redesign. This work is my evidence.

Who I am

I am Kym Primrose, an experience designer with over 15 years of working knowledge behind me.

My work and interests tend to span multiple disciplines associated with human behaviour, lived experience, user experience and accessibilty. I hold an undergraduate in Computer Arts, a MSC in Interaction Design and have since held in-house, agency and freelance roles. My own personal experience of neurodiversity has lead to a deep understanding of the true meaning of accessiblity and in particular, contextual disabilty.

Creativity has been a constant. Starting with drawing and illustration, expanding into graphic design, and eventually settling on user experience and human-computer interaction. The common thread across all of this is a genuine curiosity about how people interact with things, how the wider environment can shape experience and how accessibility is contextual.

Outside of design, writing and sharing my thoughts has become a meaningful part of my life. The focus tends to land on the intersection of human behaviour, technology, philosophy and ethics. Particularly around AI and what we risk losing if we hand too much of the creative process to systems that replicate rather than understand.

There is a fine balance between AI as an accelerant and AI as FOMO (fear of missing out) integration. Just because you can, does not always mean you should.

"If we want AI to enhance our work rather than replace the heart of it, we’ve got to be intentional, we’ve got to be aware of what we’re doing. Shneiderman’s Prometheus Principles make this clear: high automation can support creativity and oversight, if we build it that way." — Kym Primrose, UX Collective, 2025

02

My journey

My professional journey has moved between freelance, in-house and agency roles, across large corporations and start-ups. This exposure means I am familiar with office politics, the reality of product design and the constraints that come with each environment.

I started as a traditional artist using traditional mediums, transitioning into my first role with NCR as a UI designer, pushing into digital product territory, with all the constraints and rigour that comes with designing at scale for real users within the R&D devision of a large corporation. I then started my own freelance practice Designed by Kym, focusing on a wide mix of art, product and graphic design eventually leading to my role as UX designer for 3D mapping company WRLD. WRLD focused on digital twins and office booking systems for clients like Heathrow Airport and Bloomberg. I then moved on to a UX role at Vidatec, a software design agency when I have worked in almost every sector. I’ve worked with clients like Saga, the NHS, Crystal Ski and most recently the MOD. It is with Vidatec that I moved from UX design to a hybrid Product Owner and UX Strategist role.

My working relationship with AI as a tool is intentional and considered. I am knowledgable about the systems behind LLM agents, how they gather and interpret data and how impactful they are on the environment. This is why I believe that it’s important to know when to utilise AI and when to rely on more traditntal methods. I am absolutely pro-AI but there is a balance requiresd to be able to utilise it sustainably.
